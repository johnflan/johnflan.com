--- title: RightTurn Android application created: !!timestamp '2012-09-01 15:39:00' header: proj_right_turn_header.jpg --- {% mark excerpt -%} Right turn was the Android based interface to a social traffic aggregation system. This was created as part of group project work during my time in [Imperial College](http://www3.imperial.ac.uk/computing/). The overall concept of the project was to integrate Tweets about traffic in London with data feeds from [Transport for London](http://tfl.gov.uk), in order to expose social knowledge around traffic events. {%- endmark %} ##System overview The backend system collected tweets from Twitter and TfL data feeds which were stored in a geographic database. To identify valuable tweets; tweets which discussed traffic, we trained a [naive Bayes classifier](http://wikipedia.org/wiki/Naive_Bayes_classifier) to perform that task. Of these identified tweets it was discovered that only a small number of tweets contained explicit geographic information (GPS coords). To maximize the value of the tweets without geographic information, we attempted to use [reverse geocoding](http://wikipedia.org/wiki/Reverse_geocoding). This involved a process of creating a regular expression to identify street and place names and utilised the [Google Maps API](https://developers.google.com/maps/documentation/geocoding/#ReverseGeocoding) to resolve those names to geographical locations.
